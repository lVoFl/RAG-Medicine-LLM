\begin{chineseabstract}

随着人口老龄化和生活方式变化，慢性病人群规模持续扩大，高血压、高血糖和高血脂等“三高”相关健康咨询需求不断增加，传统线下问诊在连续性和及时性方面存在一定限制。近年来，大语言模型快速发展，在自然语言理解与生成任务中展现出较强能力，也推动了智能问答系统在医疗健康领域的应用探索。

但在医学场景中，直接使用通用大语言模型仍存在明显不足，例如知识幻觉、证据依据不清、风险提示不足以及回答稳定性不够等问题，难以满足医学问答对事实准确性、可追溯性和安全边界的要求。针对这些问题，本文围绕“三高”慢性病咨询场景，设计并实现了一套基于检索增强生成（Retrieval-Augmented Generation, RAG）的医学问答系统，并引入直接偏好优化（Direct Preference Optimization, DPO）进一步提升回答质量。

本文研究内容主要包括四个方面：一是构建面向“三高”主题的医学知识库，对多源医学文本进行清洗、格式统一、语义切分和元数据组织；二是设计BM25与向量检索结合的混合召回策略，并引入重排机制提高证据相关性；三是通过Prompt与上下文结构化组织，将检索证据稳定注入生成过程，并采用LoRA+DPO训练优化模型在事实表达、完整性与安全边界方面的输出偏好；四是完成前后端分离的系统实现，构建业务服务、模型服务和知识库管理模块，支持多轮问答、流式输出、引用展示和索引更新。

实验结果表明，混合检索在召回覆盖与相关性上优于单一路径检索，DPO优化后模型在偏好胜率和综合评分上均优于基础模型，并在事实正确性、信息完整性和风险样本控制方面表现更稳健。总体来看，本文方案能够在保持交互效率的同时提升医学问答的准确性、可解释性和可用性，可为垂直医学问答系统的设计与实现提供参考。

\chinesekeyword{慢性病医学问答；检索增强生成；混合检索；大语言模型；系统设计与实现}
\end{chineseabstract}

\begin{englishabstract}

With population aging and lifestyle changes, the number of patients with chronic diseases continues to grow, and consultation demands for the “three highs” (hypertension, hyperglycemia, and hyperlipidemia) are increasing rapidly. In parallel, large language models (LLMs) have advanced quickly and shown strong capabilities in natural language understanding and generation, which has encouraged their application in medical question answering.

However, directly applying general-purpose LLMs to medical scenarios still faces clear limitations, including hallucination, weak evidence grounding, insufficient risk reminders, and unstable response quality. These limitations make it difficult to satisfy strict medical requirements on factual accuracy, traceability, and safety boundaries. To address these issues, this thesis designs and implements a medical QA system for the “three highs” scenario based on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and further improves response quality with Direct Preference Optimization (DPO).

The study includes four major parts. First, a domain-specific medical knowledge base is constructed from multi-source texts through cleaning, format normalization, semantic chunking, and metadata organization. Second, a hybrid retrieval strategy combining BM25 and dense vector retrieval is proposed, together with reranking to improve evidence relevance. Third, structured prompt and context construction is used to inject retrieved evidence into generation, while LoRA+DPO training is applied to optimize factuality, completeness, and safety-oriented response preference. Fourth, a decoupled front-end/back-end system is implemented with business service, model service, and knowledge management modules, supporting multi-turn dialogue, streaming output, citation display, and index updates.

Experimental results show that hybrid retrieval outperforms single-path retrieval in both coverage and relevance, and the DPO-optimized model consistently surpasses the base model in preference win rate and overall quality, with clearer gains in factual correctness, completeness, and risk control. Overall, the proposed system improves medical QA accuracy, interpretability, and robustness while preserving interactive efficiency, and provides a practical reference for vertical medical QA system design and deployment.

\end{englishabstract}
